    • FOOTBALL DIET

    CALORIES:

    Depending on their activity level, they should aim for 1,500 to 2,000 calories per day.

    Breakfast:

    Whole grain toast or oats
    Eggs (boiled, scrambled, or an omelet)
    A piece of fruit (banana, apple, or berries)
    A glass of milk or a yogurt smoothie

    Mid-Morning Snack:

    A handful of nuts (almonds, walnuts) or a small fruit

    Lunch:

    Grilled chicken or fish with brown rice or whole wheat bread
    A serving of vegetables (salad, steamed veggies)
    A small serving of yogurt

    Afternoon Snack:

    Grilled chicken or fish with brown rice or whole wheat bread
    A serving of vegetables (salad, steamed veggies)
    A small serving of yogurt

    Dinner:

    Grilled chicken or fish with brown rice or whole wheat bread
    A serving of vegetables (salad, steamed veggies)
    A small serving of yogurt

    HYDRATION:

    Drink plenty of water throughout the day (at least 6-8 glasses).
    Include electrolyte-rich drinks like coconut water, especially after intense training sessions.

    BEFORE TRAINING:

    A small meal or snack rich in carbohydrates and moderate in protein about 1-2 hours before training (e.g., a banana with peanut butter).

    AFTER TRAINING:

    A post-workout meal rich in protein and carbohydrates within 30-60 minutes after training to help muscle recovery (e.g., a protein shake with a piece of fruit).

    FOODS TO AVOID:

    Excessive sugary snacks and drinks
    Fried and fast foods
    Processed foods with low nutritional value
