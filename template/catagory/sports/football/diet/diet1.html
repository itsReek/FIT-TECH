    • FOOTBALL DIET

    CALORIES:

    Depending on their activity level, they should aim for 1,500 to 2,000 calories per day.

    Breakfast:

    Whole grain toast or oats
    2 scrambled eggs (boiled or omelet is also fine)
    A piece of fruit (banana, apple, or berries)
    1 glass of milk or a yogurt smoothie

    Mid-Morning Snack:

    A handful of nuts (almonds, cashews, walnuts)
    A small fruit (apple or banana)

    Lunch:

    Paneer or tofu
    Brown rice or whole wheat bread (chapati)
    A serving of vegetables (broccoli, spinach)
    1 bowl of dal
    A small serving of yogurt or 1 glass of orange juice

    Afternoon Snack:

    A piece of fruit (apple) or a protein bar
    A handful of seeds (pumpkin or sunflower)
    1 glass of milk

    Dinner:

    Lentil soup
    Lean protein: Whole wheat chapati with mixed salad (olive oil dressing)
    A serving of vegetables (steamed or stir-fried)
    1 glass of buttermilk

    HYDRATION:

    Drink 6-8 glasses of water throughout the day.
    Include electrolyte-rich drinks like coconut water after training.

    BEFORE TRAINING:

    A small snack 1-2 hours before: A banana with peanut butter or multigrain crackers.

    AFTER TRAINING:

    A post-workout meal: Protein shake with a piece of fruit (banana or apple) within 30-60 minutes.

    FOODS TO AVOID:

    Sugary snacks and drinks (candy, soda)
    Fried and fast food (french fries, burgers)
    Processed foods (instant noodles, chips)
