    • FOOTBALL DIET

    CALORIES:

    1,700 to 2,200 (depending on activity level)

    Breakfast:

    Whole grain toast with avocado
    4 scrambled eggs (boiled or omelet)
    A piece of fruit (banana or berries)
    1 glass of full-fat milk or a protein shake

    Mid-Morning Snack:

    A handful of nuts (almonds, cashews)
    Greek yogurt with honey and mixed nuts

    Lunch:

    Grilled paneer or chicken
    Quinoa or whole wheat chapati
    A serving of vegetables (carrots, spinach)
    1 bowl of dal
    A glass of fresh juice

    Afternoon Snack:

    A piece of fruit (pear or banana)
    Peanut butter sandwich
    A handful of seeds (sunflower or pumpkin)

    Dinner:

    Grilled chicken breast or paneer
    Sweet potato
    A serving of vegetables (steamed or stir-fried)
    A small salad with olive oil dressing
    1 glass of lassi

    HYDRATION:

    Drink 6-8 glasses of water throughout the day.
    Include electrolyte-rich drinks like coconut water after training.

    BEFORE TRAINING:

    A small snack 1-2 hours before: A peanut butter sandwich or multigrain crackers.

    AFTER TRAINING:

    A post-workout meal: Protein shake with a small piece of fruit (pear or banana) within 30-60 minutes.

    FOODS TO AVOID:

    Sugary snacks and drinks (candy, soda)
    Fried and fast food (french fries, burgers)
    Processed foods (instant noodles, chips)
